Automotive lighting systems have been developed as important as most cutting edge systems of new generation vehicles. The new technologies used in headlamps allow to pixelate the scene by light and project many patterns, not only limited to basic lighting beams. These modules among various other modules need to exchange data in real time keeping CAN-FD bus. Whereas the advanced lighting modules receive a large amount of photometrical images from the central unit. In order to achieve optimum data transferring, data must be highly reduced without losing its quality. This paper introduces a data compression approach that can be applied to all automotive data classes over different transmission mediums. Autoencoders are potentially proper to address this need. Encoders convert images to latent representation, and the former transits the medium to the decoder of the module to reconstruct the same image. Lossy data can also be boosted by another Autoencoder architecture to alleviate the drawbacks of compression. The performance was evaluated on a generated photometrical dataset comprising a variation of light features, of 320x80 pixels. Our model suggests a 96% compression rate, and 38 of PSNR after boosting. Experimental results are compared with JPEG too Our compression approach is lossy, nevertheless, it preserves a global image quality necessary to keep photometric regulations fulfilled. Besides all the above advantages, the compression rate that we achieve is better than the compression ratio of state-of-the-art syntactic compression methods.
